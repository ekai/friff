About the Festival

What Is FRiFF?

The Flying Robot International Film Festival is an open friendly competitive film festival that exists to promote the art of film created from the perspective of flying cameras, or drones. The festival is open to all. Submission fees are low. Prizes will be awarded. The festival is now open for submissions. The final selected videos will be screened this November in San Francisco. Winners will be notified prior to the screening.

Submissions

Call for Entries

You make good drone videos, do ya? Just got a new Phantom or Solo for your birthday? Well, why don’t you submit the best of what you got to our humble little film festival.

Why Submit?

Fame, fortune, the validation of your peers! Is that not enough?
We will also have prizes for winning prizes. That's right, money and stuff.

What to Submit?

Primary, we’re looking for video or films shot from the perspective of a flying camera that tells a story. We’re not interested in seeing what your backyard looks like. Unless your backyard is full of alligators or kangaroos or something. Videos that tell a story are more compelling than just random clips.

This doesn’t mean your work needs to exclusively contain drone footage. In fact, it can be more compelling if you have a narrative with aerial footage being a component. We don't have a hard rule on how much aerial footage needs to be included. Any you do include, should be in service to the larger story.

The one big constraint is the final video should be less than 5 minutes in length.

How to Submit?

We use a video submission management platform called FilmFreeway.com. You can find out more about them here. To submit, you’ll need to create an account on FilmFreeway and submit through this link.

Keep these deadlines in mind when submitting. We cannot accept entries after the late deadline.

  • September 1, 2015: Regular deadline
  • October 1, 2015: Late deadline
